> [!ABSTRACT] Executive Summary
> Indian agriculture is trapped in a "Low Soil Carbon, High Chemical Input" equilibrium. 
> 
> The Economic Survey 2025-26 highlights a critical distortion: The massive price asymmetry between **Urea (N)** and **P/K** fertilizers has led to a skewed application ratio of **$10.9 : 4.1 : 1$** (against an ideal of $4:2:1$). The proposed solution is a paradigm shift: move from **Product-Subsidy** (cheap Urea) to **Farmer-Subsidy** (Direct Income Transfer/Acre-based support).

---

## 1. The Diagnosis: The Distorted N-P-K Ratio

The [[Green Revolution]] was built on NPK, but the current usage pattern is ecologically ruinous.

| Period | N:P:K Ratio | Status |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| **Ideal Agronomic Standard** | **$4 : 2 : 1$** | Balanced Nutrition |
| **2009-10** | $4.3 : 2 : 1$ | Near Optimal |
| **2023-24** | **$10.9 : 4.1 : 1$** | **Critical Imbalance** |



### Why has this happened? (The Economic Signal)
It is a case of **Rational Economic Behavior** responding to distorted incentives.
* **Urea (Nitrogen):** Heavily subsidized and price-controlled by the Centre. It is "dirt cheap."
* **DAP/MOP (P & K):** Under the [[Nutrient Based Subsidy (NBS)]] regime, their prices float closer to global market rates.
* **Result:** Farmers over-apply Urea because it is the cheapest way to make plants look green quickly, even if it destroys long-term yield.

---

## 2. The Ecological Consequence: "Soil Anemia"

The Survey notes that Indian soils are suffering from:
1.  **Nitrogen Burn:** Excess Urea reduces Soil Organic Carbon (SOC).
2.  **Law of the Minimum:** Following **Liebig's Law**, yield is limited by the *scarcest* nutrient (often Micronutrients like Zinc or Boron), not the most abundant one (Nitrogen). Adding more Urea yields **Diminishing Marginal Returns**.
3.  **Groundwater Contamination:** Nitrate leaching is poisoning aquifers in Punjab and Haryana.

---

## 3. The Policy Prescription: Decoupling Subsidy

The Survey proposes a radical shift to break the cycle.

### The "Acre-Based Support" Model
Instead of subsidizing the *bag of fertilizer*, subsidize the *farmer*.

1.  **Price Correction:** Raise the retail price of Urea to reflect its true economic cost (parity with P & K).
2.  **Income Transfer:** Calculate the average subsidy a farmer uses per acre and transfer that amount directly to their bank account (via [[PM-KISAN]] framework).
3.  **Behavioral Nudge:**
    * Since the farmer now has the cash and Urea is expensive, they have a financial incentive to **use less** and **use efficiently**.
    * They will shift savings toward **Nano-Urea**, **Bio-fertilizers**, or Soil Testing.

> [!TIP] The Logic of Neutrality
> The reform is "Fiscal Neutral" (Govt spends the same) but "Ecologically Positive" (Fertilizer use becomes balanced).
