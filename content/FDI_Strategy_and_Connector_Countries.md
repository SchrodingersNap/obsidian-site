## 1. Global Context: The Investment Chill
* **Global FDI:** Declined by **11%** in 2024.
* **The Shift:** Capital is moving out of long-gestation projects (Infrastructure) into short-cycle, high-tech sectors (Semiconductors, Data Centers).
* **India's Resilience:** Despite the global slump, India ranked **4th globally** in Greenfield Project announcements.

## 2. The "Connector Country" Strategy (Box IV.3)

## 3. Case Study: The TRIPS Shock (Box IV.1)
*A lesson in turning crisis into opportunity.*

**The Crisis (1995):** India forced to sign **TRIPS** (Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights).
* *Before TRIPS:* India used "Process Patents" (Reverse engineering drugs).
* *After TRIPS:* "Product Patents" mandatory. Fear that Indian Pharma would die.

**The Response:**
Instead of dying, Indian Pharma:
1.  **Upgraded R&D:** Moved from copying to creating complex generics.
2.  **Filed DMFs:** Massive filing of *Drug Master Files* in the USA.
3.  **Result:** Became the "Pharmacy of the World."

> [!TIP] The Lesson for Today
> Just as Pharma survived the TRIPS shock, Indian manufacturing can survive current **Tariff Shocks** (e.g., US Tariffs) by upgrading capabilities rather than seeking protectionism.

## 4. Outward FDI (ODI): A Sign of Maturity (Box IV.4)
Is money leaving India (ODI) bad? **No.**
* **Trend:** Indian firms investing abroad rose to **$23.6 Bn** (FY25).
* **Top Destinations:** Singapore (27%), USA (16%), UAE (10%).
* **Significance:** It shows Indian firms are acquiring **Strategic Assets** (Tech, Brands, Energy) abroad to strengthen domestic competitiveness.
