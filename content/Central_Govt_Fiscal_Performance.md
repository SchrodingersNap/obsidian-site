## 1. Revenue Receipts: The Buoyancy Factor
* **Post-Pandemic Shift:** Revenue receipts rose from **8.5%** (Pre-Covid) to **9.1%** of GDP.
* **Driver:** **Direct Taxes**. Share in total taxes rose to **58.8%** (FY25).
    * *Non-Corporate Tax (Personal Income Tax):* Buoyancy of **1.8**.
    * *Corporate Tax:* Collections doubled (‚Çπ4.5L Cr $\rightarrow$ ‚Çπ9.8L Cr) driven by corporate profitability.
* **Non-Tax Revenue:** Boosted by RBI Dividend (‚Çπ2.68 Lakh Crore) and CPSE dividends.

## 2. Indirect Taxes & GST 2.0
* **GST Performance:** Record collections aligned with Nominal GDP (Buoyancy ~1).
* **GST 2.0 Reforms:**
    * **Three-Rate Structure:** Standard (18%), Merit (5%), Sin/De-merit (40%).
    * **Relief:** Insurance (Health/Life) exempted. Cancer drugs to 0%.
    * **Rationalization:** Inverted Duty Structure (IDS) corrected in textiles/fertilizers.
