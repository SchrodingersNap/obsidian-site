> [!ABSTRACT] The Core Thesis
> The **Political Trilemma of the World Economy**, formulated by economist **Dani Rodrik**, asserts that three distinct features of the global order cannot co-exist simultaneously. A nation must choose **two**, inevitably sacrificing the third.
> 
> The three nodes are:
> 1.  **Hyper-Globalization** (Deep economic integration)
> 2.  **National Sovereignty** (Independent Nation-State)
> 3.  **Democratic Politics** (Mass political mobilization)

---


## 1. The Three Pathways (Consequences)

Rodrik argues that since we cannot have all three, history has moved through phases where different pairings were prioritized.

### Option A: The "Golden Straitjacket"
* **Combination:** [[Hyper-Globalization]] + [[National Sovereignty]]
* **Sacrifice:** **Democracy**.
* **The Logic:** To attract global capital and integrate into the world market, the Nation-State must implement policies that markets like (low taxes, deregulation, labor flexibility).
* **The Consequence:** Domestic voters are ignored. If citizens vote for welfare or protectionism, the state ignores them to keep "investor confidence" high.
* **Examples:** Pinochet‚Äôs Chile, China (Post-1979), The Gold Standard Era (19th Century).

### Option B: The "Bretton Woods Compromise"
* **Combination:** [[National Sovereignty]] + [[Democratic Politics]]
* **Sacrifice:** **Hyper-Globalization**.
* **The Logic:** Countries prioritize their own social contracts and domestic stability. They trade with the world but maintain "Capital Controls" and tariffs to protect local jobs.
* **The Consequence:** Limits the speed of global growth but ensures social stability and strengthens the welfare state.
* **Examples:** Post-WWII Era (1945-1975), [[Keynesian Economics]], India (Pre-1991).

### Option C: "Global Federalism"
* **Combination:** [[Hyper-Globalization]] + [[Democratic Politics]]
* **Sacrifice:** **National Sovereignty**.
* **The Logic:** If markets are global, governance must be global. We remove the nation-state and create supranational institutions that are democratically accountable to the *world* population.
* **The Consequence:** The Nation-State dissolves. Politics becomes global.
* **Examples:** The [[European Union]] (in theory, though it suffers from a "Democratic Deficit").

## 2. The Modern Crisis: Why Rodrik Matters Now

The article and current discourse highlight that the **Neoliberal Era (1990-2008)** attempted to push for Hyper-Globalization while pretending we could keep Sovereignty and Democracy. This failed, leading to:

1.  **The Populist Backlash:** Voters in the US ([[Donald Trump]]) and UK ([[Brexit]]) felt the *Golden Straitjacket* was too tight. They chose to reclaim **Sovereignty** and **Democracy** by rejecting Globalization (Tariffs, Border Controls).
2.  **The Euro Crisis:** Greece tried to keep Globalization (Eurozone) and Democracy, but lost Sovereignty (Troika dictates).
3.  **The Rise of Protectionism:** The shift toward [[De-globalization]] and "Friend-shoring" is essentially a return to a modified *Bretton Woods Compromise*.
