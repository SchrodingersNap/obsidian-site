The global economy is no longer driven by "Efficiency" but by **Security**. The Survey highlights the shift from *Economic Policy* to *Economic Statecraft*.

## 1. Economic Statecraft: The New Weapon
* **Definition:** Using economic means (Trade, Finance, Tech) to achieve *strategic* or *military* ends.
* **Historical Context:** From the *Megarian Decree* (Athens) to modern Semiconductor bans.

| Feature | **Economic Policy** | **Economic Statecraft** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Goal** | Growth, Inflation Control | National Security, Coercion |
| **Tools** | Interest Rates, Taxes | Sanctions, Export Controls, Blacklists |
| **Logic** | Efficiency | Strategic Leverage |

## 2. India's Response: "Strategic Indispensability"
India cannot just rely on resilience. It must become **Indispensable**.
* **Strategy:** Be so critical to Global Value Chains (GVCs) that coercion becomes too costly for the aggressor.
* **Action Plan:** Dominate [[Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI)]], Pharmacy, and High-end Manufacturing.
