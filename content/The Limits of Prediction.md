> [!ABSTRACT] Executive Summary
> In his seminal 1955 essay *Degrees of Explanation*, **Friedrich Hayek** challenges the "Scientistic" view that all fields of study can be modeled like Physics.
> 
> He introduces the distinction between **Simple Phenomena** (where we can predict specific events, e.g., planetary orbits) and **Complex Phenomena** (where we can only predict *patterns*, e.g., evolution or market crashes). The core thesis is that in complex systems (society, biology, economics), our knowledge is fundamentally limited to **"Pattern Prediction"** rather than "Specific Prediction."

---

## 1. The Core Distinction: Simple vs. Complex

Hayek argues that the scientific method is not monolithic. It changes based on the number of variables involved.

### A. Simple Phenomena (The Physics Model)
* **Definition:** Systems with few variables that can be isolated and measured.
* **Capability:** We can create a **Full Explanation**.
* **Example:** Ballistics. If you know the angle, velocity, and wind, you can predict *exactly* where the cannonball will land.
* **Testability:** Highly falsifiable. If the ball lands elsewhere, your theory is wrong.

### B. Complex Phenomena (The Biological/Social Model)
* **Definition:** Systems with minimum variables so large that they cannot be fully measured or solved.
* **Capability:** We can only create an **Explanation of the Principle**.
* **Example:** [[Evolution by Natural Selection]].
    * We know the *principle* (survival of the fittest).
    * We *cannot* predict exactly which species will evolve in 50 million years.
* **The Constraint:** We are limited by our "Ignorance of the Particulars." We know the rules of the game, but not the position of every piece.

## 2. The Trap of "Scientism"

Hayek critiques **Scientism**â€”the slavish imitation of the method and language of Science (Physics) in fields where it doesn't belong (Economics/Sociology).

> [!WARNING] The Pretence of Knowledge
> When economists try to predict GDP to the decimal point (like physicists predicting an eclipse), they are engaging in a "Pretence of Knowledge." They are treating a Complex Phenomenon as a Simple one.

* **Algebraic Constraints:** In a market, there are millions of equations (individual desires) to solve simultaneously. No supercomputer (or Central Planner) can solve this.
* **Result:** Attempts to fundamentally control complex systems (like Central Planning or aggressive Social Engineering) usually fail because they assume a "Degree of Explanation" that is impossible to achieve.

## 3. Pattern Prediction vs. Specific Prediction

Since we cannot know the specific outcome in complex systems, what *can* we know?

* **Pattern Prediction:** We can predict the *character* of the outcome, but not the specific event.
    * *Example:* "If you impose a price ceiling, shortages will occur." (Pattern Prediction - **Valid**)
    * *Example:* "If you impose a price ceiling, the price of bread will be exactly â‚¹40 on Tuesday." (Specific Prediction - **Impossible**)
